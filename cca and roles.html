    • My CCA & Roles in School!

    I was a dedicated member of the National police Cadet Corps (NPCC) from 2020
    to 2023. I was appointed as the Chairperson of the unit in 2022 after the CCA was
    passed down from the Secondary 4s who stepped down to my squad.

    As the Chairperson, I was tasked to plan the weekly CCA schedule in details
    according to the gameplan, liaise with my officers regaring my members'
    concerns,and to mentor my members. Most importantly I have to ensure that
    I make democratic decisions and to be the pillar of support for my peers.
    Aside from that, I was also in charge of Secondary 1 cadets where I taught them
    drills, campcraft techniques and guided them for other NPCC related projects.

    My biggest takeaway from my leadership journey was to COMMUNICATE and
    recognise that I wasn't managing the unit alone, but together with my squad
    members. I thought being the chairperson meant that I had to contribute the
    most to ease my members' burden ,however, it led to an autocratic leadership
    style that was not ideal and it caused some conflicts within the squad.

    Many activities or competitions were restricted due to the COVID-19 pandemic
    and we had to adapt to online CCA and to have the discipline to learn drills
    ourselves. I presented my CCA in 2023 for a motivational talk on how we adapted
    and stayed resilient. I participated in the E-Crime Scene Investigation which
    taught me to be meticulous as every small detail matters. After the restrictions
    loosened I attended the 2 days kayaking course which challenged me to get out
    of my comfort zone, I obtained the 1 Star Kayaking award.
    I got to taste the fruits of my labour in 2023 and achieved the Station Inspector
    rank, the highest attainable rank for a NPCC cadet. I also received a few other
    awards which is shown under the "Achievements" tab.
